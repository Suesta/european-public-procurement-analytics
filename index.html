TED Contract Award Notices · 2021–2023

Where does European public procurement money go?

An exploratory visualization of public contract awards in Europe, using TED data enriched with CPV categories.

Overview

This project explores how European public procurement awards are distributed by year, buyer country, contract type and CPV sector. The visualization is based on TED Contract Award Notices from 2021 to 2023. The original files were cleaned and aggregated before visualization in order to keep the final web page clear and easy to navigate.

The goal is not to build a complete procurement analysis system, but to provide a readable interactive overview of the main patterns in the selected data.

Interactive controls

Use the control below to switch between the economic value of awards and the number of awards.

1. Evolution over time

This chart shows the overall evolution between 2021 and 2023. It helps check whether the total volume of procurement changed strongly across the selected period.

2. Main buyer countries

This ranking highlights the countries whose contracting authorities concentrate the largest procurement volume in the filtered dataset.

3. Main CPV sectors

CPV codes classify the object of public contracts. Here they are aggregated by CPV division to make the main sectors easier to compare.

4. Contract type by year

This chart compares supplies, services and works. It is useful for seeing whether the structure of procurement changes across the period.

5. Supplier country information

The dataset does not always identify the supplier country. For this reason, the visualization separates domestic awards, cross-border awards and awards with unknown supplier country.

Main reading

The visualization suggests that the awarded value is concentrated in a limited number of buyer countries and CPV divisions. Romania, France, Italy, Poland and Spain appear among the main buyer countries in the processed dataset.

By contract type, supplies represent the largest share of the total awarded value in the three years analysed. At sector level, medical equipment and pharmaceuticals is the most prominent CPV division, followed by construction work and architecture, engineering and planning services.

The supplier country information must be interpreted carefully, because the dataset does not always identify the supplier country. For this reason, the visualization separates domestic awards, cross-border awards and awards with unknown supplier country.

Data source: TED Contract Award Notices, 2021–2023. Values are shown after basic data cleaning and aggregation. The most extreme 0.5% of award values were excluded to reduce distortion in the exploratory charts.
